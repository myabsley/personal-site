It's All About The Process

Discuss & Determine a Shared Vision

Whether working as an individual or as part of an organization, creative projects involve a client for whom the work is being done. I believe it is important to have a conversation with the client to establish a common end goal and the general guiding path for the project. The meandering in between will occur naturally, but allowing the client to play a role in the outlines of the project is important.

Understand the Problem

At the root of all projects, I see a design problem that will require critical thinking, inventive problem solving, and a deep understanding of the task at hand. I approach each assignment in the same mindset as I would a Physics problem; while the end result of such problems will be different, each requires careful consideration as well as a thorough analysis of the techniques and tools available at my disposal to solve the problem. I begin each project with a decomposition of the the problem space to fully appreciate the problem I must solve.

Brainstorm

Often, it is the bizarre combinations of ideas that lead to the best creations and innovations. The ability to brainstorm without judgment and bounce thoughts off one another is an excellent method of generating unique solutions to a complex problem. A brainstorm session is necessary, and is never complete without post-it notes!

Design & Prototype

After selecting the most appropriate solution from the brainstorm and considering exactly how the device will function, I move towards the visual design to focus on the form. After every small change to form or function, I justify why the change is necessary and how it will impact the user. In order to be able to communicate my ideas to a client or other stakeholders not directly involved in the design process, I create physical & digital prototypes and mock-ups in order to be able to demonstrate.

Revisit & Review

Iteration is a key aspect of design, and has played a large role in the projects I have worked on so far. Completing an iteration of the cycle and returning to examine a prototype several days later allows me to evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of a design with a keen and critical eye. Improvements can always be made, and it is at this stage where I must decide which changes to implement and express why, while balancing cost, usability and visual appeal.

Tasks

Designing a website to display Shawn's jewellery necessarily included large images to show off the unique and creative quality of her pieces. Tasks
